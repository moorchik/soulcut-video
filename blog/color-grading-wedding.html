Color Grading for Wedding Films

Feb 1, 2025

Rec.709, cinematic LUTs, and keeping skin tones natural across different lighting.

Wedding footage often mixes natural light, indoor venues, and different cameras. A consistent grade ties everything together and gives the film a cinematic feel without making skin tones look off.

Rec.709 and delivery

Most wedding films are delivered in Rec.709. Work in a color-managed pipeline so your export matches what clients see on screens and social. If you use LUTs, choose ones designed for Rec.709 or build a base correction before applying a look.

Skin tones first

Protect skin tones in your grade. Use secondary correction or masks when you need to push the rest of the image without affecting faces. Warm, natural skin reads as premium; green or magenta shifts read as a mistake.

Matching mixed sources

When you have multiple cameras or lighting conditions, match shots in the same scene first, then apply your overall look. Consistency within a sequence matters more than a bold grade that jumps from shot to shot.
