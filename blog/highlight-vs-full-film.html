Highlight vs Full Film: What to Deliver

Jan 20, 2025

When to offer a short highlight, a full film, or both — and how to structure your packages.

Couples and videographers often ask: should we do a highlight, a full film, or both? The answer depends on the couple's priorities and how they plan to watch and share the film.

Highlights (3–6 minutes)

Best for sharing on social, at parties, or when they want a tight emotional story. Ideal when the couple wants one "hero" piece that captures the day without the full ceremony or reception. Great for teasers and save-the-dates too.

Full films (30–60 minutes)

Full coverage of the day: ceremony, speeches, first dance, and key moments. Couples who want to relive the full day or share with family often choose this. It's more footage to edit but delivers a complete record.

Both

Many clients want both: a highlight for sharing and a full film for the archive. Packaging them together (as we do with our Highlight + Full Film option) often gives the best value and satisfies everyone.
